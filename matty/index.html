Me and Matty have been sports-betting degenerates together since high school — going on 20 years now. It’s basically our social circle at this point… the group chat, the trash talk, and the constant sweating of games we have no business watching.

A few months back, while everyone was arguing about the early slate, I’m in the chat talking about hitting a Bitcoin block. They clown me for it, obviously. Then Matty hits me with:
“Bro… I kinda want in on that.”

And that was the moment everything changed.

He buys a miner.
Then another guy buys one.
Then another.
And suddenly I’m running unpaid tech support every weekend for grown men who still can’t figure out how to screenshot on their phone.

Now every Sunday I’m juggling Red Zone with:

“Mike, my miner’s stuck at 0.0 TH/s!”
“Mike, how do I flash firmware?”
“Mike, what’s a WebUI?”
“Mike, why does mine sound like a jet engine? Is it supposed to do that?”

Meanwhile I’m just trying to watch the damn game.

But honestly? Watching my friends get hooked on mining has been hilarious. It’s like watching my bulldog Bruiser try to jump on the couch — awkward, confused, determined… and somehow they still get there eventually.

And now half of them care more about block height than the actual score.
