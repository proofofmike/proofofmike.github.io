Cooling & airflow

Noctua fans (quiet + effective)

  • Noctua NF-A14 PWM (140mm) – my go-to 140mm fan for quieter, higher-airflow miner setups.
  • Noctua NF-F12 PWM (120mm) – great when you need a strong 120mm fan with good static pressure.
  • Noctua Chromax line (black) – if you want something a little less “Noctua brown” on your desk.

Thermal paste / pads

  • Arctic MX-6 / MX-5 thermal paste – easy to apply, solid performance for ASIC repaste jobs.
  • Thermal Grizzly Kryonaut – overkill for a lot of things, but excellent if you’re really dialing in temps.
  • 0.5–1.0mm thermal pads – handy if you’re doing any VRM / memory cooling tweaks on DIY setups.

Power: Mean Well PSUs & Kasa smart control

Mean Well 12V PSUs

Always double-check your miner’s voltage/amp draw and leave headroom. Don’t run these at 100% of rated wattage.

  • Mean Well LRS-150-12 (150W, 12V, 12.5A) – nice option for a single lower-power miner.
  • Mean Well LRS-200-12 (200W, 12V, ~17A) – comfortable headroom for one NerdQaxe++ class miner.
  • Mean Well LRS-350-12 (350W, 12V, 29A) – good if you’re powering one or two small miners from a single supply.
  • Mean Well LRS-600-12 (600W, 12V, 50A) – serious headroom if you’re planning to run multiple devices or expand later.
  • Mean Well LRS-50-5 (50W, 5V) – handy 5V supply for side projects / control boards.

Kasa smart plugs & strips

  • Kasa HS300 Smart Power Strip (6-outlet, energy monitoring) – great when you want per-outlet control + power stats on a small rack.
  • Kasa KP303 Smart Power Strip (3-outlet) – smaller strip, nice for a desk setup with a few miners.
  • Kasa KP115 / KP125 Smart Plug (energy monitoring) – my preferred single-plug option for per-miner remote reboot + power draw.

Fuses, pigtails & wiring

5×20mm fast-blow fuses (for NerdQaxe++ / Bitaxe builds)

Always match the fuse type (fast-blow) and stay within safe current ratings for your board and wiring.

  • 5×20mm Fast-Blow Fuse – 5A – common stock rating on many boards.
  • 5×20mm Fast-Blow Fuse – 6A – a middle-ground option some builders prefer.
  • 5×20mm Fast-Blow Fuse – 8A – popular community upgrade for better inrush handling on NerdQaxe++-class rigs.

Pigtails & connectors

  • US 3-prong AC plug pigtail (1m, 18 AWG) – for bringing AC safely into Mean Well PSUs.
  • 5.5 × 2.1mm 90° barrel jack male pigtail (1m, 18 AWG) – handy if you’re working with gear that takes barrel connectors.
  • XT30 female pigtail (1m, 18 AWG) – for clean, modular DC connections between PSUs and miners.

Cleaning & small tools

  • Dust-Off compressed air (multi-pack) – what I use every couple of months to blow dust out of the heatsinks and fans.
  • Small electronics cleaning brush kit – nice for gently brushing heatsink fins and PCB edges.
  • USB-C cables (good quality, short length) – for firmware flashing and local console access.

Safety note

Anything power-related deserves extra caution:

  • Always confirm voltage and current requirements for your miner.
  • Leave realistic headroom on your PSU instead of running it at 100%.
  • Use appropriately sized wire (gauge) and proper connectors.
  • If you’re not confident around mains power, get help from someone who is.
