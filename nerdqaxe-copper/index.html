Living with it day-to-day

Noise and heat are the two big things that kill most home-mining dreams. With this box, both are manageable for me:

  • Noise: At my normal 40-something percent fan speed, it blends into the room. My keyboard is louder. If I mute everything and really listen, I can hear it — but it’s not the kind of sound that takes over the space.
  • Heat: I keep the room around 71 °F in the winter and 70 °F with A/C in the summer. The extra heat from this miner is noticeable if I leave the door closed for a long time, but it’s not overwhelming. It’s more “small space heater on low” than “blast furnace.”

I’ve also got dogs that shed and tween-kid-level dust in the house. The NerdQaxe++ Coppers haven’t been fussy about any of that. Every few months I shut things down, let them cool for a bit, take them outside, and hit them with short bursts of compressed air. Ten to fifteen minutes of downtime and they’re back online.
