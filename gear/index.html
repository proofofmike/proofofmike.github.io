Fuses & protection

NerdQaxe++ boards ship with an 8A Littelfuse NANO2 (2410) fast-blow fuse from the factory. I don’t list the 8A version here because most people are fine just leaving the stock fuse in place. This section is only for folks intentionally overclocking and looking at a higher-amp option.

10A option for OC builds

  • Littelfuse NANO2 10A (0451010.MR / 0451010.MRL) – the 10A NANO2 fuse people commonly use on overclocked NerdQaxe++ boards. This only raises the fuse limit — it does not mean the VRM, traces, or ASIC can safely pull 10 amps. Make small changes and keep an eye on temps and stability.

Important: Overclock at your own risk. A bigger fuse simply stops the fuse from being the first thing to blow. It doesn’t raise the actual hardware limits of the miner.
