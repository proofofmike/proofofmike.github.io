Miner gear I actually use (and recommend)

This is the exact cooling, power, smart plugs, fuses, and tools I use on my Bitaxe and NerdQaxe setups at home. No meme coins, no random junk — just stuff that keeps rigs quiet and stable.

Cooling & airflow

Noctua fans (quiet case fans)

  • Noctua NF-A14 PWM (140mm) – go-to 140 mm fan when I want max airflow without a jet engine.
  • Noctua NF-F12 PWM (120mm) – strong 120 mm fan with good static pressure for tighter builds.

AC Infinity cabinet / spot cooling

  • AC Infinity MULTIFAN S1 (80mm USB) – single 80 mm USB fan for small hot spots.
  • AC Infinity MULTIFAN S3 (120mm USB) – simple 120 mm USB fan for shelves / open racks.
  • AC Infinity MULTIFAN S5 (dual 80mm USB) – dual 80 mm for pointed airflow at VRMs / hotspots.
  • AC Infinity MULTIFAN S7 (dual 120mm USB) – dual 120 mm for cabinets or behind multiple miners.

Thermal paste & pads

Paste

  • Arctic MX-6 thermal paste – easy to work with, forgiving, and plenty good for ASIC repastes.
  • Thermal Grizzly Kryonaut – higher-end paste if you’re really chasing a few extra °C.

Pads

  • ARCTIC TP-3 100×100×1.0 mm pad – big sheet you can cut down; stackable up to 2 mm.
  • Thermal Grizzly Minus Pad 8 (120×20×1.0 mm) – nice for VRMs / narrow components.
  • Gelid GP-Extreme 80×40×1.0 mm pad – 12 W/mK pad, good gap filler for random hotspots.

Power & smart control

Mean Well PSUs

  • Mean Well LRS-600-12 (600 W, 12 V, 50 A) – beefy 12 V supply when you’re running multiple miners.
  • Mean Well LRS-350-12 (348 W, 12 V, 29 A) – good middle-of-the-road PSU for a couple of smaller rigs.
  • Mean Well LRS-200-12 (200 W, 12 V, 17 A) – lighter duty option when you don’t need huge headroom.

Kasa smart plugs & strips

  • Kasa HS300 smart power strip (6 outlets + 3 USB) – six individually switchable outlets; perfect for a small miner farm.
  • Kasa KP115 smart plug (with energy monitoring) – single-outlet plug I use to watch draw on individual miners.
  • Kasa KP125M (Matter, 2-pack) – newer Matter-compatible plugs if you’re going that route.

Cords & adapters

  • XT30 male/female pigtail set (5 pairs, 16 AWG) – XT30 pigtails for hooking miners to your PSU safely.
  • 2.1×5.5 mm DC barrel pigtail (1 male + 1 female, 18 AWG) – classic 12 V barrel leads; handy for side projects.
  • Amazon Basics 3-prong PC power cord (6 ft, 18 AWG) – basic IEC C13 cable for PSUs / supplies.

Fuses & protection

  • BOJACK 5×20 mm 5 A fast-blow fuses (pack of 10) – what I keep around for NerdQaxe++ boards that use 5 A.
  • BOJACK 5×20 mm 8 A fast-blow fuses (pack of 10) – 8 A version if your board uses the larger fuse.

Cleaning, tools & misc.

  • Dust-Off DPSXL 10 oz canned air – quick blasts outside every few months to keep fins from clogging.
  • Anti-static nylon brush kit (set of 8) – for gently cleaning boards and fans without static drama.
  • Plug-in watt / power meter – simple wall meter to verify real-world power draw.
  • Thermal Grizzly TG spatula set (3-pack) – makes spreading paste way less annoying.
  • iFixit Essential Electronics Toolkit – the driver / bit kit I grab for basically every miner job.
  • Amazon Basics USB-C to USB-A 2.0 cable (6 ft) – easy C-to-A cable for flashing devices / connecting to miners.

How I’d start (quick kits)

If you’re just trying to keep a couple of Bitaxe / NerdQaxe units happy on a desk and don’t want to overthink it:

  • Cooling: one Noctua NF-F12 per hot miner, plus an AC Infinity S7 if the whole area runs warm.
  • Power: a single LRS-200-12 or LRS-350-12 depending on how many rigs you’re running.
  • Control: put miners or PSUs on a Kasa HS300 strip so you can bounce power from your phone.

I’ll keep this page updated as I add more miners or find better gear. If something here ever turns out to be trash, I’ll pull it.

Heads up (not advice)

Double-check voltage, amperage and fuse ratings for your exact hardware. Don’t blindly copy my setup if your miner expects something different. When in doubt, ask the vendor or the Bitaxe / NerdQaxe communities before you plug anything in.
