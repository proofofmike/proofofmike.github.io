Power & smart control

Mean Well PSUs (Bitaxe & NerdQaxe power)

I use Mean Well PSUs at home; below are three common 12 V models that come up a lot for Bitaxe / NerdQaxe-style miners.

  • Mean Well LRS-600-12 (600 W)
  • Mean Well LRS-350-12 (348 W)

Note: Mean Well LRS supplies have a high inrush current at startup. Keep them on a regular outlet, not smart plugs.

Fuses & protection

NerdQaxe++ boards ship with an 8A Littelfuse NANO2 (2410) fast-blow fuse. Stick with 8A unless you fully understand the risks of pushing past factory limits.

Factory fuse (8A)

  • 8A Littelfuse NANO2 replacement (IX Tech) – the stock fuse rating for most NerdQaxe++ boards.

Overclocking fuse (10A)

  • Littelfuse NANO2 10A (0451010.MR / 0451010.MRL) – this is the correct 10A NANO2 fuse people use for OC setups. A bigger fuse does not mean your VRM, traces, or ASIC can actually handle 10 amps. Increase slowly and watch temps.

Important: Overclock at your own risk. A higher-amp fuse only removes the fuse as the first point of failure — it does not increase the safe limits of the miner.

Quick starter notes

Noise

If your miner is still running a loud stock fan, upgrading to a Noctua is the biggest quality-of-life improvement you can make.

Power

For one or two devices, the stock bricks are fine. If you're running multiple rigs or pushing OC, that's when a Mean Well PSU makes sense.

Control & monitoring

Smart strips and plugs let you monitor power at the wall and remotely power-cycle rigs.

Maintenance

This page stays updated as I test new hardware. If something turns out to be junk, I pull it.

Heads up (not advice)

Double-check wattage, amperage, and fuse ratings for your hardware. When in doubt, ask the community or check docs before wiring up a PSU or swapping fuses.
