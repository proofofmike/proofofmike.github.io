Bitaxe & NerdQaxe gear I use — plus parts recommended to me that check out

These are Bitaxe / NerdQaxe miner accessories I either use myself or that have been strongly recommended and vetted as solid options: Noctua and AC Infinity fans, Mean Well power supplies, Kasa smart plugs, fuses, tools, and a few extras that keep a small solo-mining corner quiet and stable.

Cooling & airflow

Noctua fans (quiet case fans)

  • Noctua NF-A14 PWM (140mm) – great 140 mm option when you want strong airflow on a Bitaxe or NerdQaxe setup without a jet engine.
  • Noctua NF-F12 PWM (120mm) – solid 120 mm fan with good static pressure for tighter builds or front-mounted Bitaxe rigs.

AC Infinity cabinet / spot cooling

  • AC Infinity MULTIFAN S1 (80mm USB) – single 80 mm USB fan for small hotspots and focused airflow.
  • AC Infinity MULTIFAN S3 (120mm USB) – simple 120 mm fan for shelves or open racks near miners.
  • AC Infinity MULTIFAN S5 (dual 80mm USB) – dual 80 mm for pointing straight at VRMs or ASIC hotspots.
  • AC Infinity MULTIFAN S7 (dual 120mm USB) – dual 120 mm for cabinets or behind multiple Bitaxe / NerdQaxe miners.

Thermal paste, pads & heatsinks

Paste

  • Arctic MX-6 thermal paste – easy to work with, forgiving, and plenty good for ASIC repastes.
  • Thermal Grizzly Kryonaut – higher-end paste if you’re chasing a couple of extra °C margin.

Pads

  • ARCTIC TP-3 100×100×1.0 mm pad – big sheet you can cut down; stackable up to 2 mm to bridge gaps.
  • Thermal Grizzly Minus Pad 8 (120×20×1.0 mm) – good for narrow VRM strips and small components.
  • Gelid GP-Extreme 80×40×1.0 mm pad – 12 W/mK pad, solid gap filler for hot components near the hash board.

Copper heatsinks (aftermarket)

  • GeeekPi 18-piece pure copper heatsink kit – the standard “home miner” aftermarket upgrade kit. High-quality copper sinks that perform noticeably better than cheap aluminum ones. A lot of my devices came with copper from the factory, but if you’re upgrading after the fact, this type of kit is what you’d use.

Power & smart control

Mean Well PSUs (Bitaxe & NerdQaxe power)

I use Mean Well PSUs at home; below are three common 12 V models that come up a lot for Bitaxe / NerdQaxe-style miners. You don’t need all of them — pick what matches your power budget and number of rigs.

  • Mean Well LRS-600-12 (600 W, 12 V, 50 A) – beefy 12 V supply when you’re running multiple miners off one PSU.
  • Mean Well LRS-350-12 (348 W, 12 V, 29 A) – good middle-of-the-road Mean Well for a couple of miners with headroom.
  • Mean Well LRS-200-12 (200 W, 12 V, 17 A) – lighter-duty option when you don’t need a huge shared PSU yet.

Kasa smart plugs & strips

  • Kasa HS300 smart power strip (6 outlets + 3 USB) – six individually switchable outlets; perfect for a small Bitaxe / NerdQaxe “farm”.
  • Kasa KP115 smart plug (with energy monitoring) – single-outlet plug I use to watch draw on individual miners.
  • Kasa KP125M (Matter, 2-pack) – newer Matter-compatible plugs if you’re in that ecosystem.

Cords & adapters

  • XT30 male/female pigtail set (5 pairs, 16 AWG) – XT30 pigtails for safely connecting miners to your Mean Well PSU.
  • 2.1×5.5 mm DC barrel pigtail (1 male + 1 female, 18 AWG) – classic 12 V barrel leads; handy for Bitaxe/Hex and side projects.
  • Amazon Basics 3-prong PC power cord (6 ft, 18 AWG) – basic IEC C13 cable for powering your Mean Well or other PSUs.

Fuses & protection

  • BOJACK 5×20 mm 5 A fast-blow fuses (pack of 10) – what I keep around for boards that use 5 A 5×20 mm fuses (some NerdQaxe++ variants).
  • BOJACK 5×20 mm 8 A fast-blow fuses (pack of 10) – 8 A version if your specific board / PSU fuse is rated higher.

Cleaning, tools & misc.

  • Dust-Off DPSXL 10 oz canned air – quick blasts outside every few months to keep fins and filters clear.
  • Anti-static nylon brush kit (set of 8) – for gently cleaning boards and fans without static drama.
  • Plug-in watt / power meter – simple wall meter to verify real-world power draw at the outlet.
  • Thermal Grizzly TG spatula set (3-pack) – makes spreading thermal paste way less annoying and more consistent.
  • iFixit Essential Electronics Toolkit – the driver / bit kit I grab for basically every miner job.
  • Amazon Basics USB-C to USB-A 2.0 cable (6 ft) – easy C-to-A cable for flashing or connecting to miners and dev boards.
  • HT1 smart temperature & humidity sensor – easy little temp sensor to sit near your rigs. If the ambient temperature around them spikes, you want to know. I’m actively exploring automations to pair this with Kasa smart strips so I can trigger alerts and, eventually, automatic shutoffs if things get too hot.

Quick starter kits

Noise:
If your miner is still running a loud stock fan, upgrading to a Noctua is the biggest quality-of-life improvement you can make. It dramatically cuts noise while keeping temps stable. In my opinion, absolutely worth it.

Power:
For one or two devices, the stock power bricks are usually fine. If you’re pushing heavy overclocks or running multiple Bitaxe / NerdQaxe units, that’s when a Mean Well PSU becomes the right move. Once this hobby grabs you and you start adding rigs, switching to a shared PSU setup just makes everything cleaner and more efficient.

Control & monitoring:
Using a smart strip lets you monitor power at the wall, schedule on/off times, and remotely power cycle rigs if something freezes. Super convenient, especially when you’re away from home and want to bounce a miner without touching the PSU.

Maintenance:
This page gets updated whenever I add new miners, test new accessories, or find better gear. If something here ever turns out to be trash, I’ll pull it — no fluff, no bag-holding.

Heads up (not advice)

Double-check voltage, amperage and fuse ratings for your exact hardware. Don’t blindly copy my setup if your miner expects something different. When in doubt, check the Bitaxe / NerdQaxe docs or ask the community before wiring up a new PSU or fuse.
