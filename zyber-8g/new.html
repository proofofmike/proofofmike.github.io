Real results from my desk

This Zyber 8G Premium was run at stock settings and allowed to settle. The important part is not peak spikes. It is how flat and boring the line looks once everything warms up.

Across a full day: 10 TH/s average, about 137 W, 13.21 J/TH observed, and stable thermals. No fan hunting and no weird behavior.

Efficiency that actually stands out

At 13.21 J/TH observed, the Zyber 8G is operating in a very efficient zone for a home miner at this hashrate level. For context, this is meaningfully more efficient than the NerdQaxe++ units I run on my desk.

They are different classes of hardware, but it highlights what Zyber is optimized for: delivering more hashrate per watt without pushing fans or thermals hard.

Noise and daily comfort

This unit is running on my desk, next to my laptop, with a TV mounted on the wall on the other side of the room. I have no problem hearing the TV at a reasonable volume.

The fans are audible, but steady, and they do not ramp aggressively. Once it settles, it stays there. Honestly, I would not even have an issue running this in a bedroom.

Why Zyber 8G exists

There is a gap in home mining. Smaller open-source desk miners are quiet but limited. Full-size ASICs bring noise, heat, and power complexity.

Zyber 8G sits in the middle: multi-ASIC performance with power delivery and thermals designed to make sense in a room where people live.
