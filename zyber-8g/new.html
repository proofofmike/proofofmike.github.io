Efficiency that stands out

At 13.21 J/TH observed, Zyber 8G is operating in a very efficient zone for a home miner at this hashrate level. For context, this is meaningfully more efficient than the NerdQaxe++ units I run on my desk.

Noise and daily comfort

This unit is running on my desk, next to my laptop, with a TV mounted on the wall on the other side of the room. I have no problem hearing the TV at a reasonable volume.

The fans are audible, but steady, and they do not ramp aggressively. Once it settles, it stays there. Honestly, I would not even have an issue running this in a bedroom.

A quick reality check on home mining odds

I’m seeing a lot of people jump into home mining lately, so I want to briefly cover what you can expect from devices in this class.

At time of writing (Feb 09, 2026), Bitcoin difficulty is about 125.86T and estimated network hashrate is about ~1.02 ZH/s. These odds are based on stock hashrates and current network difficulty. Source links: difficulty and hashrate.

Here is what the annual odds look like in plain English. This is the chance of finding at least one Bitcoin block in a year:

  • Zyber 8G (10 TH/s): about 1 in 1,715 per year
  • NerdQaxe++ (6.1 TH/s): about 1 in 2,811 per year
  • Bitaxe Gamma 601 (1.2 TH/s): about 1 in 14,285 per year

A lot of the “lottery miners” you see advertised are 1000 kH/s or less. That is roughly 1 MH/s, which works out to about 1 in 17.1 billion per year at this difficulty. That is why I focus on real TH/s-class home miners.

These miners are designed for solo mining. Yes, you technically can join a pool, but with miners this small the payouts are usually minimal. You are talking pennies a day if that, and that is before factoring in your electrical cost.

People do find blocks with these. It happens. They use the same class of ASIC chips industrial miners use. The difference is scale. Industrial farms run hundreds of thousands of chips. A Zyber 8G runs 8.

You can also mine other SHA-256 coins (BCH, DGB, etc.). I do not mine those and I do not cover them here.

Why Zyber 8G exists

There is a gap in home mining. Smaller open-source desk miners are quiet but limited. Full-size ASICs bring noise, heat, and power complexity. Zyber 8G sits in the middle: multi-ASIC performance with power delivery and thermals designed to make sense in a room where people live.
